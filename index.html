Self-Assessment Letter

Dear DCDA Faculty,

My name is Summer Regan. I am a senior double majoring in Strategic Communication and Digital Culture and Data Analytics, and this semester I completed my internship at Agency Habitat in Fort Worth as a Paid Media Intern. This letter is meant to give you the context you need to understand the work I have included in this portfolio and why I chose it.

I want to start by saying that I am genuinely proud of this semester. Not because everything went smoothly or because I always knew what I was doing, but because I showed up to something that pushed me in a real direction and I did not shy away from it. I came into this role having already interned at the agency last summer in a content capacity, but paid media is a different world. It is more analytical, more precise, and more demanding in ways I did not fully anticipate. That gap between what I expected and what I encountered is where most of my growth happened.

My day-to-day responsibilities included boosting and managing paid social posts across Meta and TikTok, contributing to media strategy research, sitting in on client and internal meetings where I offered paid media input, and collaborating with the brand and content teams. I also helped wherever I was needed, which at an agency means a lot of things. One week that might mean pulling performance numbers for a reporting deck. Another week it might mean researching competitor ad formats or helping draft outreach emails. I learned early on that being useful in this kind of environment requires adaptability, and that ended up becoming one of the things I am most proud of developing this semester.

The two work samples I have included reflect the most substantive contributions I was able to document from this role. The first is a paid media strategy document I was asked to create by Carlos, VP of Media, which outlines how brands with varying budgets should approach platform selection, campaign objectives, KPIs, and format decisions. Building this required me to synthesize everything I had been absorbing over the semester and translate it into something practical and usable for the agency. The second is an AI visibility and GEO audit I developed after spending time researching how generative engine optimization is changing the way brands need to think about their digital presence. This one felt especially aligned with my DCDA background because it sits directly at the intersection of digital culture, data, and strategy. Both documents represent the kind of work I could not have produced at the beginning of this semester, which is why I chose them.

My daily log reflects the full arc of the internship, from early weeks where I was absorbing terminology and learning how to navigate the systems, to later weeks where I was contributing independently and taking on projects with less direct oversight. I included it because I think it shows the texture of what this experience actually looked like, including the meetings, the research, the small tasks, and the moments where things clicked.

In terms of the course itself, my discussion posts this semester reflected both sides of who I am as a student. Some of them were more analytical, drawing on what I was observing at the agency and connecting it to broader trends in digital media and data. Others were more personal and reflective, especially as the semester went on and I started to understand more clearly what I was actually learning. I tried not to treat the posts as checkboxes. I used them to think through things I was genuinely curious about, and I think that comes through in the writing.

If I had to make a case for my grade, I would point to three things. First, the quality and relevance of the work samples I produced, both of which required original research, strategic thinking, and the ability to communicate ideas clearly. Second, the consistency with which I showed up, engaged, and adapted throughout the semester. And third, the fact that the agency asked me to extend through August, which I think speaks to the kind of intern I was.

This internship confirmed that I want to work at the intersection of strategy, data, and digital media. I came in curious about the analytical side and I am leaving more certain than ever that it is where I belong. This portfolio is my attempt to show that work honestly and completely.

Summer Regan

Questionnaire

Prompt 01 — Advice for New Interns

Do not wait until you feel ready, because that moment may not come. The best thing you can do going in is be honest about what you do not know and stay curious enough to figure it out. Pick an internship that actually challenges you, not just one that looks good on paper. If you can, choose a place where you will be exposed to more than one function. I came in as a content intern and came back doing paid media, and that range has taught me more than staying in one lane would have. On the course side, anything that touches analytics, digital platforms, or campaign strategy will serve you well before you step into a role like this. And if I could tell myself one thing going back, it would be to ask more questions earlier and worry less about seeming like you do not know things. Nobody expects you to.

Prompt 02 — How the Internship Changed How I Think

Being in this role made a lot of things from my DCDA classes feel real in a way they did not before. I had learned the acronyms and understood the concepts, but seeing how a campaign actually performs, and watching how small decisions affect real numbers for real clients, made the data feel a lot more human. It is not abstract anymore. Every metric represents someone choosing to engage or scroll past, and that shift in perspective has changed how I look at content I consume too. I am more aware now of the strategy behind what I see online and more critical of it. It has also made me more confident in my own instincts, because I realized the way I was already thinking about digital culture was actually applicable in a professional setting.

Prompt 03 — How the Internship Shaped My Career Plans

This internship confirmed what I already suspected, which is that I want to work at the intersection of strategy and digital media. I came in curious about the analytical side and left more certain that it is where I belong. The fact that the agency asked me to extend through August feels like a platform in the most literal sense. I am building relationships, developing fluency with industry tools, and gaining the kind of experience that is hard to fake on a resume. What I still need is to keep sharpening the technical side, whether that is deeper platform certifications, more exposure to media planning, or stronger data visualization skills. The foundation is there. Now it is about building on it.

Prompt 04 — Recommendations for the DCDA Website

The internship page would benefit from real examples of what students actually do day to day, not just job titles. When I was deciding whether this qualified as a DCDA internship, I had to piece that together myself. Testimonials from past interns, even short ones, would go a long way. It would also help to show the range of industries and agency types where DCDA students have landed, so prospective interns can see how transferable the program is beyond one type of role.

Prompt 05 — How to Improve the DCDA Program

The program would be stronger with more industry connections built in, whether that means alumni panels, agency partnerships, or just more structured networking opportunities before students are trying to find internships on their own. It would also help to be clearer upfront about what DCDA actually is and what it prepares you for. More hands-on tools training — Meta Ads Manager, Google Analytics, Looker Studio — would make students feel less behind when they step into data-facing roles. And showcasing what alumni are doing now would make the program feel more real and more worth investing in.
